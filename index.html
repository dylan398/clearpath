What We Do

We turn your project idea into a ready-to-execute package — with every contractor already committed.

Pre-construction is the most consequential phase of any project. The decisions made before a single permit is pulled — who designs it, how it's documented, which contractors are approached, how bids are structured and compared — determine whether a project comes in on budget, on time, and without surprises.

Most owners don't have the time, the network, or the process to manage that phase themselves. That's what Clearpath is for. We coordinate every piece — from the first design conversation to the last contractor signature — so you receive a complete package and make one decision: which option you want.

We work across all project types and all trades. When a project calls for architects and structural engineers, we bring them in. When it needs civil, MEP, or specialty subs, we have those relationships too. The scope of what we coordinate scales with what your project actually requires.

One Relationship Instead of Ten
Without Clearpath, an owner manages architects, engineers, a GC, and a dozen subcontractors — each with their own timeline, their own format, and their own incentives. Every one of them needs to be coordinated, followed up with, and held accountable.
Clearpath is the single point of contact for the entire pre-construction process.
Bids You Can Actually Choose Between
Unmanaged bid processes produce incomparable proposals — different scopes, different assumptions, different formats. Owners end up guessing rather than deciding. The cheapest bid is often the one that's missing the most.
We organize all bids into clear, comparable package options built for a real decision.
Contractors Committed at Handoff
Most bid processes end with numbers on paper and nothing actually committed. Getting from "approved bid" to "signed contractor" is another project entirely — and another round of negotiations the owner has to manage.
We don't hand off until contractors are committed to the selected package.
